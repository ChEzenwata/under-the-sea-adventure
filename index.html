Ocean Zones

☀️ Sunlight Zone

0–200 m deep


The sunlight zone is the uppermost 200 meters of the ocean
where sunlight penetrates, supporting photosynthesis and
sustaining the majority of marine life.

🌅 Twilight Zone

200–1000 m deep


This zone is home to a diverse array of life forms,
many of which are undiscovered by science. It plays a
vital role in the marine food web and the global carbon
cycle.

🌑 Midnight Zone

1000–4000 m deep


Sunlight does not penetrate this depth, which makes
photosynthesis impossible and leaving the zone in complete
darkness. Many species in this zone have bioluminescent
abilities to attract prey, communicate or lure mates.

⚫ Abyssal Zone

4000–6000 m deep


Water temperatures are extremely cold in this zone, typically
0-4°C. Life here relies on marine snow or chemosynthesis near.
This zone is largely unexplored, offering potential, ecological,
and biomedical discoveries.

🕳️ Hadal Zone

6000+ m deep


Exploring the Hadal zone is extremely difficult due immense pressure,
darkness and remoteness. Scientists use remotely operated vehicles
and autonomos underwater vehicles.

Fun Facts

🌊 More than 80% of the ocean remains unexplored.

🐟 The ocean contains millions of species.

🦑 Some deep-sea creatures create their own light.
